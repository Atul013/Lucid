The uncomfortable truth driving Horizon 1: the archive is still mostly synthetic — seeded calendar and health data, zero Telegram history imported. The intelligence layers are real; the life they reason about isn't yet.

The route

NOW — make it real

Lucid must reason about a real life, not evt_0000. Everything here unblocks; nothing here is new surface area.

1.1
Real data over demo data. Purge seeds, sync real Calendar, run the Telegram history import. One item that upgrades every AI layer at once.
1.2
Oracle ARM deployment. Always-on backend + HTTPS. A passive twin can't live on a laptop that sleeps; keeps the ₹9,569 credit untouched.
1.3
Land the in-review train. WhatsApp ingestion + formatting, Local Notes, console UI, onboarding. Six open PRs is inventory, not progress.
1.4
Scheduler. Nightly ego refresh, drift check, 7am briefing, SNN feed. "It just shows up" is the product promise.
1.5
Data-freshness panel. Per-connector last-sync + record counts, so stale data is visible and 1.1 never silently regresses.
NEXT — close the loop

From "answers questions about you" to "tells you things you didn't ask."

2.1
Weekly Review. Auto Sunday retro: wins, drift deltas, relationship changes, spend vs. forecast, next-week risk from the twin.
2.2
Memory resurfacing. "One year ago today" + abandoned intentions brought back at the right moment.
2.3
Relationship nudges. "You haven't replied to X in 12 days; you usually reply within 1."
2.4
Remaining connectors. Google Keep, Notion, Discord — plus new candidates: browser history/screen-time, Spotify, photos EXIF.
2.5
Eval harness. Golden questions per AI layer, run in CI — the only defense against silent quality regressions.
2.6
Cost telemetry. NIM token usage + per-feature spend on a /system page, before budget becomes a problem.
2.7
Mobile on-device LLM. llama.rn + quantized small model, hybrid routing. Blocked on native tooling.
2.8
Secret auto-redaction. Scrub OTPs, account numbers, passwords at ingestion — some things shouldn't be remembered even encrypted.

Principles · solo guardrails

  1. Vertical slices. Every branch ships something visible end-to-end; no layer-only work.
  2. Mock-first, real-second. Every connector works keyless in mock mode, so demos never depend on credentials.
  3. Privacy is a feature, not a section. Encryption, export, purge and redaction ship with features, not after them.
  4. The archive is the product. Connectors and AI layers are replaceable; the accumulated, owned memory is not.
  5. Hardware waits. The rule that opened the old roadmap still closes this one.
  6. PROGRESS.md discipline. An idea isn't real until it has a row and a branch. This page holds ideas; that file holds truth.
